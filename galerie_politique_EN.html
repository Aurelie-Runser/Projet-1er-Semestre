Elizabeth 1er

Years: 1533-1603 (76 Years)
Nationality: British
Occupation: Queen of England
Best known for: be the Queen of England

Elizabeth 1st ascended the throne when England was in civil war between Catholics and Protestants. At first, she tried to calm this conflict, and then she stopped the wars with the other countries.

Her reign spanned 44 years. Indeed, thanks to her intelligence, diplomac, and courage, she escaped assassination attempts. During her reign, there was a cultural and artistic boom, with military victories and religious stability for the first time in a long time in England.

I know I have but the body of a weak and feeble woman, but I have the heart and stomach of a king, and of a king of England...

Eleanor Roosevelt

Years: 1884-1962 (78 Years)
Nationality: American
Occupation: Humanitarian & Political
Best known for: the 1st First Lady of the United States to really invest in her job

Eleanor Roosevelt was very present in the politic. She was changing the position of First Lady, which was an extra.

She supported the black American and women's rights movements, fought against child labour, for a shorter working week and for a minimum wage... Her actions were controversial, but Eleanor Roosevelt did not stop there.

Between 1946 and 1952, she was a member of the United Nations and was the first Chairperson of the United Nations Commission on Human Rights and took an active part in the elaboration of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights.

After 1953, same she was no longer in politics, she was still very active, working for women's rights.

Do what you feel in your heart to be right – for you’ll be criticized anyway.

Sirimavo Bandaranaike

Years: 1916-2000 (84 Years)
Nationality: Sri Lankaise
Occupation: Politician
Best known for: be the 1st woman in democratic history to be elected to head a government

While Sirimavo Bandarabaike was organising social initiatives, her husband, the Prime Minister, was assassinated in 1959. She replaced him in 1960. So she became the 1st woman in the world to be elected Prime Minister. She had conducted a socialist and nationalist policy.

After, she was re-elected in 1970, and she created a new constitution, giving birth to the Republic of Sri Lanka. She introduced economic measures to redistribute the riches, although the economic plan did not change.

Sirimavo Bandarabaike had a controversial career, between the exclusion of the Tamils, her sometimes ineffective measures and her accusations of corruption, however she allowed Sri Lanka to emerge from colonialism, made numerous reforms and was a woman of influence during a period of political instability.

Madeleine Albright

Years: 1937-
Nationality: American
Occupation: Politician
Best known for: to be the first woman Secretary of State in the United States

Madeleine Albright's father was a diplomat, so she learned a lot about international relations. After being a journalist and a political consultant, Madeleine Albright became in 1993 the US ambassador to the United Nations. Her merit was recognised by her seriousness, her firmness and her accountability during complex and delicate negotiations.

In 1996, she became the 1st woman Secretary of State in the United States. She was very active during her mandate: she negotiated peace in the Middle East and China, she influenced the positions of North Korea and Eastern Europe in not making/using atomic bombs… Also, she was the first US Secretary of State to go to North Korea!

Madeleine Albright broke the glass ceiling for women in American politics. She was known for her business sense, her diplomacy and her ability to calm internationalconflicts. She inspired many women to enter politics!

Benazir Bhutto

Years: 1953-2007 (54 Years)
Nationality: Pakistani
Occupation: Politician
Best known for: be the first woman Prime Minister of Pakistan

Benazir Bhutto has studie in Harvard then Oxford, and was president of a debate association.

In 1978, she became the co-chair of the centre-left Pakistan People's Party. Later, she went into exile but returned to Pakistan in 1985 when martial law was ended. In 1988, after the elections, she became Prime Minister. She was the 4th woman in history to be at this position, and the first in a majority Muslim country.

Although she was elected, many conservative Muslims refused to see a woman in power.

She established a policy to reduce unemployment and poverty. Unfortunately, it did not succeed in reducing gender inequality.

Benazir Bhutto, despite the difficulties, was a great and determined leader with a strong character. She is an example of charisma and influences many women.
