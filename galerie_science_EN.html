Ada Lovelace

Years: 1815-1852 (36 Years)
Nationality: British
Occupation: Mathematician
Best known for: being the world's first woman programmer

Ada Lovelace was talended naturally with numbers and codes. Hes mother encouraged her to study this science and found teachers to teach them her.

During her studies, she met many academics, notably Charles Babbage. This man wrote mechanical calculator’s plans, then analytical machinery’s plans, which solve more complex calculations. Ada was fascinated about this computer prototypes.

When she transleted Babbage’s works in english, she added his personal notes with an algorithm. Ada Lovelace was differente from other scientists, because she was convinced we could do more thing mit analytical machinery than just calculations.

Thanks she, science was advance in the field of information technology, at the point today a Lovelace medal exists, to give poeple who do advance computer systems.

His name, Ada, is too a programming language to honor her.

Elizabeth Blackwell

Years: 1821-1910 (89 Years)
Nationality: British
Occupation: Doctor
Best known for: be the 1st woman to receive a medical diploma in the United States in 1879

During the 19th century, be a girl and be educated was a miracle. But Elizabeth Blackwell was decided to become doctor. She send applications to many school and the Hobart and William Smith College in Geneva, in Geneva, at New York, agreed but they think it was a jok. Studies’ Elizabeth were very difficult but she she successfully graduated in 1879. After, she work in London and Paris.

In 1857, she created, with her sister and a doctor, a clinic for New York poor families called New York Infirmary for Women and Children. Years later, the establishment offered formations for woman who want do medicine.

Despite her health problems, Elizabeth Blackwell milita same at the end of her life for women's rights and to give who wanted it access to care by women medical staff.

Elizabeth Blackwell showed woman and man were equal in medicine. This observation is became normal nowadays.

Clara Barton

Years: 1821-1912 (91 Years)
Nationality: American
Occupation: Teacher, Nurse & Humanitarian
Best known for: creat the American Red Cross in 1881

Clara Barton was a teacher. When the Civil War beginin, she decided to give her help to soliders. In firt, she provided foods in 1861. In 1962, she became nurs. Her nickname was “the angel of the battlefield”.

In 1870, she participated in the International Committee for the Relief of Wounded Soldiers (formerly the International Committee of the Red Cross) during the Franco-German War.

In 1881, Clara Barton creat the Red Cross in United States and was ths 1st president.

Amelia Earhart

Years: 1897-1937 (40 Years)
Nationality: American
Occupation: Aviator
Best known for: having achieved many records in the field of aviation

Amelia Earhart had a strange childhood for the time, because she was educated like a boy. She believed that womens were just as good as men, so they could also achieve great things. She inspired a lot of women to realize their dreams.

After a first flight in 1920, she fell in love with aviation.

She was the 1st woman who travel the Atlantic in plane in 1928, same during this flight, she was an extra.

In 1932, she released her dream and became the 1st woman and the 2nd person who do a transatlantic flight alone. She received a medal for this.

She released other records, for woman or two sexe. In 1937, she decided to do the round-the-world flight. No one had done it before. Unfortunately, 29 June 1937, the coastguards lost Amelia Earhart and her aircraft co-pilot in Pacific Ocean. The plane and bodies were never found.

Women, like men, should try to do the impossible. And when they fail, their failure should be a challenge to others.

Sally Ride

Years: 1951-2012 (61 Years)
Nationality: American
Occupation: Astronaut & Astrophysicist
Best known for: be the 1st American woman who in the space

Sally Ride was selected in 1978 by NASA, which form astronaut then they were one thousand applicants. After many years of intensive training, she was chosen to do the space shuttle Challenger mission in June 1983. So, she is the 1st American woman who goes into space and has an important place in the team. In October 1983, she returns in space as a mission specialist.

She leaves NASA in 2001 and create a company, Sally Ride Science. This society aims to inspire children to learn science and make it fun and interesting.

I don't know why, but I never gave in to the stereotype that science was not for girls. My parents encouraged me to do it. Unlike many of my friends, I never listened to teachers or school counsellors.
