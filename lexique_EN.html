A

Analytical machine
programmable calculating machine designed in 1834 by the English mathematician Charles Babbage (Wikipedia)
Anglo-Dutch War
series of four wars in the 17th and 18th centuries between the Kingdom of Great Britain and the 7 provinces of the Spanish Netherlands (Wikipedia)

C

Champion of the Earth
British mathematician and visionary inventor during the 19th century, one of the main precursors of computing. He was the first to formulate the principle of a computer (Wikipedia)
Civil War
American civil war between 1861 and 1865 (Wikipedia)
Clinic
(masculine noun) establishment where free consultations and medical services are provided (Le Robert)

F

Force Française Libre (FFL)
armed forces of France loyal to General de Gaulle during the Second World War
Franco-German War
war between France and the coalition of German states from 1870 to 1871 (Wikipedia)

H

Hobart and William Smith College
2 private liberal arts colleges, one for girls and one for boys, located in Geneva, New York, established in 1797. The colleges offer Bachelor of Arts, Bachelor of Science and Master of Arts degrees in teaching. For more informations: https://gaz.wiki/wiki/fr/Hobart_and_William_Smith_Colleges

M

Maquis
(masculine noun) in the Resistance, maquis refers to resistance fighters hiding in places where there is little traffic, like forests and mountains
Martial law
a legal state of exception, in which the army maintains order in place of or in collaboration with the army (Wikipedia)

R

Red Cross
international humanitarian organisation created to help victims of war (Larousse
Reformist
(non-masculine) political doctrines aimed at changing existing economic and social structures for the better by gradual changes in laws rather than by revolution (Wikipedia)
Reservist
a soldier who comes to reinforce the ranks voluntarily
Rifleman-voltiger
shooter with a shotgun placed in the 1st line to trouble the enemy

S

Suffragette
(feminine noun) woman who, first in Great Britain, militated for the right of women to vote (Le Robert

T

Tamil
(adjective and noun): people of south-eastern India

U

Underground Railroad network
a network of clandestine roads and safe havens used by escaped African-American slaves (Wikipedia)

V

Votes for Women
a newspaper associated with the women's suffrage movement in the United Kingdom, published from 1907 to 1918. It was the official newspaper of the Women's Social and Political Union until 1912 (Wikipedia)

W

Women’s Social and Political Union (ou WSPU)
(or WSPU): a feminist organisation founded in 1903 and disbanded in 1917 where suffragettes militated in favour of women's right to vote in the United Kingdom (Wikipedia)
Women's pay
French law showing women's salary lower than men's
