The book They Changed the World was written by Lewis Carrie and translated from English by Chantal Mitjaville. This book, published in 2019, describes 60 women who have played or are playing an important role in our society. These little-known women come from all parts of the world, from various domains and from different periods, from the 16th century to the present.

Book format

Elles ont changé le monde is a succession of portraits of women who have had an impact, in one way or another, on our world.

They are classified into 5 categories: Mathematics, Science and Technology, followed by Literature, Fashion and Art, then Activists and Revolutionaries, hen Women in Power and Politicians, and finally Sport and Entertainment.

In every domain, more or less women are known, making it possible to learn more about them. Others are much less well known. This book gives you the opportunity to learn more about important women.

Each girl is accompanied by a photo of her, or a painting if photography did not yet exist, as well as pictures of their actions or awards.

All descriptions are presented in the same form. First, the woman's first and last name. The subheading is her date of birth and death. Her nationality and activity are given too. The text consists of two parts. First, there is an introduction in the form of a paragraph. It explains how this woman changed the world. Secondly, the majority of the text is a biography. The latter covers the period from her childhood to her death, focusing on her actions.

Who exactly are these 60 women?

The girls presented are not necessarily feminists. They have played an important role in history, in small and large ways.

There are these who proved that a woman could succeed in a domain considered masculine, such as Marie Curie in science, Elizabeth Blackwell in medicine, Aphra Behn in literature, Sirimavo Bandarabaike in politics or all the great sportswomen.

Others have marked the world with their lives, Coco Chanel, Rosa Parks and Anne Frank for example.

Some inspire many people with their generosity and their humanitarian acts, Clara Barton, who saved many men from war, Michelle Obama and her investment for women and blacks or Diana Spencer and her multiple humanitarian actions.

On the other hand, some of the women presented are active feminists. Suffragettes in England or Marie Stopes, the founder of family planning, are perfect illustrations.

If you are interested in the portraits of women, you can look in the Gallery. Thes are arranged in categories and are short, simple and precise. So you can easily and quickly learn about the woman of your choice.
