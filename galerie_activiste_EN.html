Harriet Tubman

Years: 1820/1822-1913 (93/91 Years)
Nationality: American
Occupation: Abolitionist activist
Best known for: activist for the abolition of African-American slavery

Born slaver, Harriet Tubman was convinced African-American, or black, weren’t lower white.

In 1849, she successfully escaped and arrived in Pennsylvania, a state where slavery had been abolished. So, she was free. However, she wasn’t satisfied. She decided to join the Underground Railroad network and she led a slave liberation expedition in 1851. Shed did 18 shipments when she succeeds in saving her family.

After the Civil War, she became a spy for the party in favour of the abolition of slavery. So she was the 1st woman who advises an assault group. Thanks to his valuable information, the assault group was able to free 700 slaves.

Later, Harriet Tubman fought sexism and racism.

Hariiet Tubman was nicknamed “Moses”. Her struggles and her determination inspired many African-American and many women.

Emmeline Pankhurst

Years: 1858-1928 (69 Years)
Nationality: British
Occupation: Politician
Best known for: creating the suffragette movement and helping to establish women's right to vote in the United Kingdom

Emmeline Pankurst created in 1903 the la Women’s Social and Political Union, an organisation claiming the right to vote for women in the United Kingdom. The activists call suffragettes.

Emmeline Pankhurst organised speeches, demonstrations, rallies and a newspaper (Votes for Women) and parliaments with only women.

Emmeline Pankhurst was first arrested in February 1908 when she tried to deliver a protest to Prime Minister H. H. Asquith in Parliament. During her trial on 21 October 1908, shedeclared:

We are here not because we have broken the law, we are here because we want to make the law.

During the World War 1, she pressed the government to women can replace men in the workplace.

Women were accorded the right to vote in 1918, but only for those over 30 years of age. After Emmeline Pankhurst's death, the right to vote, was given to women over 21, like men.

Massoumeh Ebtekar

Years: 1960-
Nationality: Iranian
Occupation: Activist, Scientist & Politician
Best known for: be the first woman vice-president of the Republic of Iran

Massoumeh Ebtekar became the 1st woman vice-president of the Republic of Iran in 1997. She is responsible for the environment and undertakes many important reforms.

A feminist, she leads a network of women's NGOs in Tehran, the capital of Iran, and dares to criticise the conduct of some men in Afganising.

In 2017, in her new mandate of vice-president, she was put in charge of women's and family affairs.

In 2006, Massoumeh Ebtekar received the Champion of the Earth award, for his commitment to environmental change as a member of the Iranian government.

She has many ambitious projects, defends her positions, whether political, environmental or religious, and never stops trying to advance the domains in which she intervenes.

Malala Yousafzai

Years: 1997-
Nationality: Pakistani
Occupation: Blogger & Activist
Best known for: received Nobel Peace Prize in 2014

Malala Yousafzai was born in a reformist Pakistani family and she could go to school. She doesn't agree with taliban's and she is engaged in women's rights. This is very evident on his blog. In 2012, she was the target of an assassination attack and was shot in the head. The world was in shock. She received treatment and was able to continue living.
