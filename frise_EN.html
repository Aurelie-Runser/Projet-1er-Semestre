1713: Anne-Marguerite Petit du Noyer, French, First known journalist

1737: Emilie du Chatelet, French, First woman to obtain a scientific publication

1740: Laura Bassi, Italian, first woman to obtain a university teaching diploma inscience

1843: Ada Lovelace, English, First person to write a computer program

1849-53: Elizabeth Blackwell, American, First lady doctor

1880: France, girls accepted to go to college and high school

1893: New Zealand gives women’s right to vote, First pays which do this

1896: Zelia Nuttal, American, First woman film director

1901: Jeanne Chauvin, France, First Lawyer

1907: Finlande, First legislative election in the world where women are elected

1918: Armenia + Hungary + Canada + United Kingdom + Poland + Georgia + Azerbaijan + Austria + Germany + Estonia + Latvia + Lithuania give women the right to vote

1922: Amelia Earhart, American, First woman to cross the Atlantic

1936: France, 1st women in government : Cécile Brunschvicg, Suzanne Lacore, Irène Joliot-Curie

1944: France give women's right to vote

1946: France, abolition of the law on women's pay

1953 : Vijaya Lakshmi Pandit, Indian, First minister, ambassador, woman president of the UN General Assembly

1960: Sirimavo Bandaranaike, Sri Lankan, First Prime Minister of a country

1963: Valentina Terechkova, Russia, First woman in space

1965: France, Law authorising women to exercise a professional activity without the authorisation of the husband. Law allowing women to open a bank account in their name

1974: Isabel Martinez de Peron, Argentina, First President of a country

1980: Vigdis Finnbogadottir, Icelandic, First president of the republic elected by universal suffrage

2011: Saudi Arabia gives women's right to vote, the last country to do this
